Staghorn fern

Platycerium bifurcatum · mounted on a reclaimed oak board

Bright indirect Epiphyte Statement

£38

Description

In the wild, staghorns don't grow in soil at all — they cling to tree bark, catching leaf litter and rain in their shield fronds. Ours arrive already established on a reclaimed oak board, ready to hang from a single brass hook.

The antler fronds carry a silvery felt that protects them from scorch. Don't wipe it off — it's not dust, it's the plant.

Care guide

  • Light — bright and indirect; an east-facing wall is ideal.
  • Water — soak the board for 15 minutes weekly in summer, fortnightly in winter.
  • Humidity — happiest above 50%; a bathroom with a window is a fine home.
  • Feeding — half-strength liquid feed once a month through the growing season.
