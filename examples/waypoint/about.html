About Waypoint

One writer, one rucksack, no sponsors to please.

Waypoint started as e-mails home from a night train somewhere between Zagreb and the rest of the itinerary. The e-mails got long, the list of recipients got longer, and eventually it seemed kinder to give the whole thing a URL.

The rules of the house: overland where the map allows it, sleep where the route says rather than where the reviews say, and never pretend a place was empty when it was full of other people also holding cameras. Travel writing that forgets the bus queue is fiction.

The site itself is part of the journal's spirit — plain HTML pages you could read from a memory stick, styled by one stylesheet and a handful of design tokens. Even the page-to-page animation is just CSS, and switches itself off if your device asks for reduced motion.
